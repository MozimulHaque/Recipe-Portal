Cucumber Salad

A cucumber salad is a refreshing dish made primarily with cucumbers, often sliced thinly or chopped, mixed with various ingredients like onions, tomatoes, herbs (such as dill or parsley), vinegar, oil, and sometimes a touch of sweetness. It's known for its light, crisp texture and tangy, flavorful dressing. The salad is versatile, often served as a side dish or a light appetizer, and it's popular in many cuisines worldwide due to its simplicity and fresh taste.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Vegetables

Wash the cucumbers thoroughly and slice them thinly. You can use a mandoline slicer for uniform slices. Thinly slice the red onion.

Step 2: Mix the Dressing

In a mixing bowl, combine the sliced cucumbers, sliced red onions, and chopped fresh dill. In a small bowl, whisk together the white vinegar, olive oil, salt, and pepper.

Step 3: Combine and Serve

Pour the dressing over the cucumber mixture in the mixing bowl. Gently toss everything together until the cucumbers and onions are evenly coated with the dressing. Let the salad marinate in the refrigerator for about 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Serve chilled as a refreshing side dish or garnish with extra dill before serving, if desired.

ingredients

2 large cucumbers, thinly sliced

1/2 red onion, thinly sliced

2-3 tablespoons chopped fresh dill

2 tablespoons white vinegar

2 tablespoons olive oil

Salt and pepper to taste
