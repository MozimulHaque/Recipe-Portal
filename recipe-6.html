Cheese Burger

A cheeseburger is an iconic American dish consisting of a grilled or fried ground beef patty, typically seasoned and shaped into a round form, sandwiched between two halves of a soft bun. What sets a cheeseburger apart is the addition of cheese, usually melted over the beef patty during cooking. The cheese adds a creamy, savory element that complements the juicy meat. Toppings can vary widely and often include lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles, ketchup, mustard, and mayonnaise, allowing for a customizable flavor profile. It's a classic comfort food enjoyed worldwide for its satisfying combination of flavors and textures.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Beef Pattiess

Divide the ground beef into four equal portions and shape them into patties slightly larger than the size of your hamburger buns. Season both sides of each patty with salt and pepper.

Step 2: Cook the Patties

Preheat a grill or a skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the patties for about 3-4 minutes on each side for medium doneness, or adjust the time according to your desired level of doneness. In the last minute of cooking, place a slice of cheese on each patty and allow it to melt.

Step 3: Assemble the Cheeseburgers

Toast the hamburger buns lightly on the grill or in a toaster. Place the cooked cheeseburger patties on the bottom halves of the buns. Add your preferred toppings such as lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles, ketchup, mustard, or any other condiments. Complete the burgers with the top halves of the buns. Serve immediately and enjoy your homemade cheeseburgers!

ingredients

1 pound ground beef (85% lean recommended)

Salt and pepper to taste

4 slices of cheese (American, cheddar, or your choice)

4 hamburger buns

Toppings of your choice (lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles, ketchup, mustard, etc.)
