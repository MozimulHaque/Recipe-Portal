Vegetable Soup

"Vegetable soup" typically refers to a dish made by simmering various vegetables in a broth or stock. It's a versatile and healthy dish that can include a wide range of vegetables like carrots, celery, onions, tomatoes, potatoes, and more. Sometimes, herbs, spices, or noodles are added for flavor and texture. Nutritionally, vegetable soup is often low in calories and high in vitamins, minerals, and fiber, making it a popular choice for those seeking a healthy meal option. It's easy to customize based on personal taste preferences or dietary needs, and it's often considered a comfort food due to its warmth and heartiness. Overall, vegetable soup is a simple, nutritious, and customizable dish that can be enjoyed in various ways, making it a staple in many households.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

instructions

Step 1: Prepare Ingredients

Gather an assortment of vegetables such as carrots, celery, onions, potatoes, tomatoes, and any others you prefer. Chop the vegetables into bite-sized pieces. Optionally, prepare some garlic, herbs (like thyme or bay leaves), and spices for added flavor. If using store-bought broth, have it ready; otherwise, prepare homemade vegetable broth.

Step 2: Cooking

Heat a large pot over medium heat and add a bit of olive oil. Saut√© the onions and garlic until they become translucent. Add the rest of the vegetables and cook for a few minutes until they start to soften. Pour in the vegetable broth, covering the vegetables. Add herbs, spices, salt, and pepper to taste. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for around 20-30 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.

Step 3: Serve

Taste the soup and adjust seasoning if needed. Ladle the soup into bowls and serve hot. Optionally, garnish with fresh herbs or a dollop of sour cream or yogurt.

ingredients

Assorted Vegetables: Carrots, onions, celery, and potatoes (about 2 cups each, chopped)

Garlic: 2-3 cloves, minced or chopped

Vegetable Broth: 4-6 cups (homemade or store-bought)

Olive Oil or Butter: 2 tablespoons

Salt and Pepper: To taste
