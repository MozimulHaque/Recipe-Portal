Red Sauce Pasta

Red sauce pasta is a beloved dish featuring pasta coated in a rich, tomato-based sauce. Typically made by simmering tomatoes with garlic, onions, herbs (like basil and oregano), and sometimes additional ingredients like bell peppers or carrots, the sauce is then combined with cooked pasta. This flavorful and versatile dish can be customized with various ingredients such as mushrooms, olives, spinach, or protein like chicken or shrimp. It's a comforting, satisfying meal that's popular worldwide for its simplicity and robust flavors.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Pasta

Cook the pasta according to package instructions in a large pot of salted boiling water until al dente. Drain and set aside, reserving a little pasta water.

Step 2: Make the Red Sauce

In a separate pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and saut√© for about a minute until fragrant but not browned. Pour in the crushed tomatoes or tomato sauce, dried basil, and dried oregano. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Allow the sauce to simmer for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, to let the flavors meld and the sauce to thicken slightly.

Step 3: Combine and Serve

Add the cooked pasta to the sauce, tossing gently to coat the pasta evenly. If the sauce is too thick, you can add a little reserved pasta water to loosen it. Once the pasta is coated with the sauce, remove from heat. Serve the red sauce pasta in bowls, garnished with grated Parmesan cheese and fresh basil leaves, if desired.

ingredients

8 ounces of pasta (penne, spaghetti, or your choice)

2 tablespoons olive oil

3 cloves garlic, minced

1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes or tomato sauce

1 teaspoon dried basil

1 teaspoon dried oregano

Salt and pepper to taste

Grated Parmesan cheese for garnish (optional)

Fresh basil leaves for garnish (optional)
